Apply for Authorization

Thank you for using NYU's Academic Accreditation Compliance Instrument. Based on your responses, your organization should apply for authorization in New Mexico. Please see below for information and instructions about how to apply.

  • Because you are a institution, your institution is regulated by the New Mexico Higher Education Department.
  • Because your Institution is:
    • Offering programs that lead to New Mexico licensure;
    • Having seminars, conferences and/or internships;
    • Meeting for more than 40 hrs, $500, and one course in sequence;
    • Maintaining a location, site, server, phone and/or address;
    • Contracts to provide students with services;
    • State advertising;
    • Employing any faculty; and,
    • Offering distance ed to NM students and employing recruiting agents in New Mexico
  • In New Mexico, you should apply for program and/or institutional authorization with the New Mexico Higher Education Department.
  • Application Process
    • There are two types of authorization in New Mexico: Licensure and Registration. Each type of authorization has different requirements for the process to apply and fees.
    • Because you are a multi-institution system , each institution within your organization in New Mexico must apply for authorization individually;
    • Applications for authorization to New Mexico Higher Education Department must include either proof of current accreditation status (by a body recognized by BOTH the U.S. Department of Education and the New Mexico Higher Education Department) OR include a plan to become accredited within three years;
    • The initial authorization application process generally takes 6 to 9 months.
  • Because your institution is operating with a Presence in New Mexico, your institution needs to apply for a License.
