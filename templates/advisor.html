Do you employ any agents who provide organized, consistent, on-the-ground recruiting to residents of New Mexico?

What is a recruiting agent?

For New Mexico regulations, a recruiting agent is an individual paid by an institution to solicit enrollments. Select "no" if your insitution's employees or agents conduct ONLY occasional recruiting of residents of New Mexico (i.e. job fairs in New Mexico). If your agents recruit at re-occuring events, select "Yes."

Does your institution offer programs that lead to professional licensure in New Mexico?

Why is this important for state authorization?

In some cases, programs that are offered require programmatic approval in New Mexico by an external body in addition to institutional state authorization approval.

More on licensure and the New Mexico Higher Education Department (NMHED).

In addition to the programs listed that require external program approval, there are 29 Boards and Commissions Divisions that provide professional licensure and may have jurisdiction over the respective programs and institutions offering such programs. At times an institution may be under both, the oversight of NMHED and a board. If the institution is offering programs not falling under the jurisdiction of the particular board or department, responsible for the institution's oversigt, then the oversight responsibility falls to NMHED.

4. If your institution offers any of these programs that lead to licensure, select "yes": Teacher Education; Nursing Cosmetology; Acupuncture and Oriental Medicine; Massage Therapy; Private Investigations; and, Allied Health Professions.

Does your institution provide educational offerings that require students to physically meet in New Mexico for instructional purposes that can be characterized by ANY of the following:

Why is this important?

In New Mexico, any educational offering by an out-of-state institutuion will trigger a need for authorization if the educational offering requires that students meet physically AND if the offering costs $500 or more AND/OR meets for 40 or more total hours AND/OR is required as part of a sequence or series of courses.

6. Select "yes" if students are required to meet for 40 or more hours, the educational offering costs $500 or more and/or the educational offering is required as part of a sequence or series of courses.
