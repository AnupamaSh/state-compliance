Does your Institution requires State authorization from New Mexico?

According to the New Mexico Higher Education Department (NMHED), a program or institiution is considered to have a physical presence in New Mexico if it offers courses, programs or degrees on-site or from a geographical site in New Mexico, or if it maintains an administrative, corporate or other address in the state. Based on the responses your provide, your state authorization advisor will help you to determine if your program or institution should seek authorization with the New Mexico Higher Education Department, or take some other action.

What types of programs require external approval in New Mexico?

Education; Nursing; Cosmetology; Acupuncture and Oriental Medicine; Massage Therapy; Private Investigations; Allied Health Professions.

More on licensure and the New Mexico Higher Education Department (NMHED).

In addition to the programs listed that require external program approval, there are 29 Boards and Commissions Divisions that provide professional licensure and may have jurisdiction over the respective programs and institutions offering such programs. At times an institution may be under both, the oversight of NMHED and a board. If the institutionis offering programs not falling under the jurisdiction of the particular board or department, responsible for the institution's oversigt, then the oversight responsibility falls to NMHED.
